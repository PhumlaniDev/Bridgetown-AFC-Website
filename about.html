Our mission:

"Its a community team which is looking to develop young underprivileged and privileged youth, all is welcome. Our aim is to help the community develop the youth for a brighter future and opportunities."

Bridgetown AFC was established in 1991 and is celebrating 30 years of existence as a football club. Bridgetown AFC was formerly known as Bridgetown United before becoming Bridgetown AFC.

Our History

In the 1980's there was a team by the name of Crusaders that was based in Bridgetown. This team is the reason why Bridgetown AFC exists today. During their playing days, Crusaders had very good players from all over Athlone. However due to internal conflict and clash of egos, the team decided to split up. Half of the team formed what is known today as Chelsea Bridgetown FC and the other half formed Bridgetown United, which later became Bridgetown Association Football Club. The people that started the club are not in charge anymore however their are a few families that played a major role in the establishment of Bridgetown AFC. The families in particular are the Awaldiens, the Kamaldiens and the Reiners family. Bridgetown AFC had some very talented players over the years, like Riyaaz Hopkins, who represented WP as well as South Africa u21. Bridgetown AFC is a proud football association that stands as a pillar of strength in the Bridgetown community and has kept kids engaged in sport and promotes physical well being
